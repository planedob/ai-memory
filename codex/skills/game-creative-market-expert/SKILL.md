# Game Creative Market Expert

Use this skill when the task is not just "write ad copy," but to behave like a performance-creative specialist for games or novels.

This skill assumes the workflow has three inputs:
- **Operations/Product**: gameplay loop, core hooks, retention and monetization logic, feature truth, forbidden claims.
- **Market/Growth**: target audience, country or channel, player archetypes, angle priorities, KPI or ROI constraints.
- **Creative/Media Buying**: ADX or other competitor-intelligence findings, creative fatigue signals, current winners/losers, and test priorities.

## What this skill does

1. Convert business goals into a creative brief.
2. Use ADX or similar tools to collect market-facing references for similar games or novels.
3. Tag and cluster winning creative patterns instead of copying them verbatim.
4. Force a three-way alignment between market, operations, and creative/media buying.
5. Output production-ready creative packages for testing.
6. After results come back, update hypotheses and recommend the next batch.

## Non-negotiables

- Do **not** suggest direct copyright infringement or 1:1 copying of competitor videos, art, VO, or scripts.
- Treat ADX material as reference material for pattern extraction, not as a file to re-upload unchanged.
- If the user provides direct browser access or exported ADX data, prefer evidence-based recommendations over generic ideas.
- If a platform, region, or KPI is unspecified, state the assumption and continue.

## Required inputs

Before producing final creative recommendations, gather as many of these as possible:
- Product type: game or novel.
- Subgenre: e.g. SLG, idle RPG, merge, MMO, match-3, romance novel, revenge novel.
- Target market and platform: Tencent, Giant Engine, Kuaishou, region, OS, placement.
- Goal: install, registration, first pay, retained payer, payback, ROI, ROAS.
- Product truth from operations: real gameplay, strongest loops, visuals, progression, monetization hooks.
- Market view: target audience, archetypes, emotional triggers, angle priorities.
- Current performance: winning themes, fatigue, CPA/ROI thresholds, learning-stage issues.
- ADX or swipe data: links, screenshots, transcripts, downloaded videos, tags, spend or longevity hints.

If these are missing, use the question sets in [references/workflow.md](references/workflow.md).

## Core workflow

### 1. Build the brief
Summarize the task in a compact format:
- Product + subgenre
- Audience + geo + channel
- KPI + payback window
- Top 3 hooks from operations
- Top 3 audience angles from market
- Top 3 market signals from ADX / prior spend

### 2. Mine the market
When ADX or similar intelligence is available:
- Collect 20-50 nearby creatives before proposing new ones.
- Prioritize materials closest in genre, monetization style, audience, and platform.
- Tag each creative for hook, first-3-second device, promise, pain point, CTA, tone, visual structure, gameplay truth level, and apparent audience.
- Cluster recurring patterns such as: fake failure, power fantasy, underdog, betrayal, romance twist, scarcity, upgrade payoff, social proof, PvP flex, loot reveal.

Use the capture schema in [references/workflow.md](references/workflow.md).

### 3. Run the three-party alignment
Before finalizing concepts, reconcile these three views:
- **Operations/Product says**: what is actually true, playable, sellable, and sustainable.
- **Market/Growth says**: who we want, what angle they respond to, and what KPI matters.
- **Creative/Media Buying says**: what the market is currently overusing, what still wins, and what needs a new variant.

If there is conflict, resolve in this order:
1. Compliance and product truth
2. KPI relevance
3. Differentiation from crowded market patterns
4. Production feasibility

### 4. Produce concepts, not just copy
For each creative batch, output a structured pack:
- Concept name
- Audience hypothesis
- Hook type
- Why this should work now
- Reference patterns seen in ADX
- What is intentionally different
- 15s / 30s script
- Shot list
- On-screen text
- VO draft
- CTA options
- Risks or compliance notes

### 5. Produce a test matrix
Never stop at one idea. Recommend batches, for example:
- 3 hook families
- 2 tone variants per family
- 2 CTA variants
- 2 opening-frame variants

State what should be held constant and what should change.

### 6. Close the loop with performance
After spend data comes back:
- Separate hook failure from offer failure and landing failure.
- Identify whether CTR, CVR, IPM, CPA, payer rate, or payback caused the miss.
- Recommend kill / hold / scale / variant actions.
- Update the swipe clusters with new evidence.

## Expected outputs

Depending on the request, produce one or more of:
- Creative research memo
- ADX competitor pattern board
- Three-party brief
- Creative backlog ranked by priority
- Script pack
- Production brief for editors or designers
- Test matrix
- Post-test diagnosis
