# Successful Project Patterns

This file captures the common structure seen across strong performance projects on domestic paid channels. Use it to design operating systems, not one-off hacks.

## Pattern 1: One clean goal beats mixed objectives

Strong accounts optimize around one primary business event per campaign cluster.

Weak pattern:
- same campaign expected to do曝光,留资,成交,私域导流 all at once

Strong pattern:
- upper funnel for exploration
- mid/lower funnel for one explicit conversion
- separate remarketing / recapture layer

## Pattern 2: Creative throughput is a growth engine

Successful projects usually ship new creative angles continuously.

Common loop:
- weekly hypothesis list
- daily or near-daily asset refresh
- fast kill of weak variants
- reuse of winning hooks across formats

What matters:
- hook freshness
- native platform feel
- offer clarity
- landing consistency

## Pattern 3: Feedback quality determines whether automation works

Automation gets dangerous when the optimization signal is noisy.

Good automation preconditions:
- callback exists
- conversion delay is understood
- invalid leads / canceled orders can be marked
- reconciliation with CRM or order data is possible

If these are weak:
- use alerts first
- slow budget moves
- require human approval on major changes

## Pattern 4: Scaling works better as controlled expansion

Strong teams scale in measured steps.

Typical discipline:
- increase budget in small increments
- duplicate only proven structures
- do not reset too many variables simultaneously
- keep a stable control group while testing new ideas

## Pattern 5: Local-service channels need ops alignment

For 美团、支付宝、本地生活、到店类投放, ad-side wins can be fake if the store cannot convert demand.

Always check:
- store hours
- staffing and response speed
- inventory / capacity
- promo redemption experience
- review quality and complaint rate

## Pattern 6: Reporting is an action system

The best media reviews are short and decisive.

Preferred output:
- stop: where spend is structurally wasted
- scale: where constraints are budget or inventory, not efficiency
- fix: tracking, landing, callback, or merchant-side issues
- test: next creative or audience hypotheses
